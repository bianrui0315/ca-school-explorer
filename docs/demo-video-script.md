# Three-Minute Build Week Demo

This plan is designed for a 16:9, 1080p screen recording that runs between 2:50 and 3:00. The narration is approximately 400 words and is intended to be read at a calm, conversational pace.

## Recording setup

- Use a clean browser window with bookmarks, personal tabs, notifications, and autofill suggestions hidden.
- Open the [live website](https://ca-school-explorer.thrilling-fragrance.workers.dev/) before recording and use 90% or 100% browser zoom.
- Keep the cursor visible, move it deliberately, and pause briefly after each click.
- Record the voice separately if needed, then align the screen actions to the narration.
- Do not add background music unless it remains clearly below the narration.

## Complete narration

### 0:00–0:20 — The problem

When a family moves for a new job or plans the next school transition, the question is not simply, “Which school has the highest score?” They need to understand which schools are nearby, what evidence is available, how outcomes are changing, and what the data cannot tell them.

### 0:20–0:38 — The foundation

California publishes valuable education data, but it is spread across separate files and dashboards. California School Explorer turns those public records into one comparable experience, with multiple years, student subgroups, transparent denominators, reliability labels, and visible source notes.

### 0:38–1:00 — Start with a new place

The family can begin in Area Explorer with a new work address, city, or ZIP. They choose the child’s grade, a search radius, public-school type, minimum evidence coverage, and the evidence priorities that matter to them, such as academic performance, attendance, school climate, or college and career preparation.

### 1:00–1:20 — Open the sample

For this demo, I will use the one-click Porter Ranch sample. Normally, a family reviews nearby results and selects up to three schools. The sample opens the same final brief immediately, so a first-time visitor can understand the value without first learning the interface.

### 1:20–1:44 — Review the shortlist

The brief starts with three schools and the context behind the search. Each card shows distance, district, grade span, school type, and evidence coverage. It also explains the strongest weighted evidence for that school. The evidence score summarizes the selected priorities, but it is explicitly labeled “not a rank.”

### 1:44–2:08 — Compare current evidence

The latest-evidence table places the schools side by side across eight indicators: English Language Arts, mathematics, chronic absenteeism, suspension, graduation, dropout, A–G completion, and the College/Career Indicator. Every value keeps its actual reporting year and reliability state. Missing or suppressed data stays missing; the project never guesses a value.

### 2:08–2:28 — See direction over time

The three-year section makes direction visible without hiding the underlying values. Families can see where evidence is improving, stable, declining, or incomplete. They can then open the full comparison for deeper subgroup analysis, including English learners and students with disabilities.

### 2:28–2:48 — Explain the evidence and limits

The brief uses deterministic rules to identify the strongest evidence, the largest differences, and important data gaps. No runtime AI rewrites official data. The warning remains visible: nearby does not mean assigned or eligible, so families must confirm boundaries and programs directly.

### 2:48–3:00 — Close

The result can be shared or printed. California School Explorer is open source, built, tested, documented, and deployed with Codex and GPT-5.6 during OpenAI Build Week.

## Editing notes

- Keep the opening title on screen for no more than two seconds before showing the product.
- Use simple cuts and one gentle zoom on the latest-evidence table; avoid decorative transitions.
- If the recording runs long, shorten pauses before removing narration. The first sentence to cut is the optional subgroup sentence in the three-year section.
- End with the live URL and GitHub repository visible for at least two seconds.
