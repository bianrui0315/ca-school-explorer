# California School Explorer v0.4.1

Release date: 2026-07-15

## Added

- A dedicated Area Explorer route at `/area`, connected to the comparison workspace through clear desktop and mobile navigation.
- Official 2025–26 CDE district polygons for exact California street-address matches.
- Distinct unified, elementary, and high-school boundary styling with an on-map legend.
- Nearby and full-district focus controls so families can inspect both local school evidence and the broader district footprint.
- A compact district context panel next to the enlarged map and grade-band evidence results below it.

## Changed

- Introduced a more polished pearl, cobalt, teal, and violet visual system with layered page backgrounds, stronger panel boundaries, refined shadows, and more generous spacing.
- Increased the Area Explorer map footprint and separated its workflow from the denser school comparison dashboard.
- Added validated, display-simplified GeoJSON to the existing privacy-preserving district lookup response.

## Data and privacy

- The underlying public bundle remains 2,878,340 observations across 9,946 schools and 1,023 district baselines.
- Address and coordinate requests remain same-origin, bounded, and `no-store`; the project does not persist submitted addresses.
- District geometry comes from the same official CDE 2025–26 district-area point lookup used for jurisdiction matching.

## Known limitations

- A district-area match is not an assigned-school result. Individual attendance areas, transfer rules, and enrollment eligibility require current official local district sources.
- Display-simplified district outlines are contextual and are not suitable for surveying, parcel, legal, or enrollment-boundary decisions.
- City and ZIP searches continue to use approximate centers and therefore do not request a district-area match.
